Protein Guide

Protein is one of the essential macronutrients your body needs to build and repair muscles, support healthy hair and skin, and produce vital enzymes and hormones. Getting the right amount of protein every day helps improve energy, boost recovery after exercise, and maintain a healthy metabolism.

How Much Protein Do You Really Need?

Protein requirements vary by age, sex, and activity level. The Recommended Dietary Allowance (RDA) for a sedentary adult is 0.8 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight per day. Active individuals, athletes, and people trying to build muscle may need 1.2-2.0 grams per kilogram of body weight per day. Use the table below as a quick guide:

Group Protein per Day Notes
Sedentary Adult (Men) ~56 g Based on 70 kg average weight
Sedentary Adult (Women) ~46 g Based on 57 kg average weight
Active Adults / Athletes 1.2 - 2.0 g per kg body weight Varies with training intensity
Teenagers ~0.85 g per kg body weight Needed for growth & development
Older Adults ~1.0 - 1.2 g per kg body weight Helps maintain muscle mass

Protein-Rich Foods

Plant-Based Protein Sources


  • Lentils & Beans (Rajma, Chana, Masoor) - Excellent protein + fiber combo.
  • Chickpeas - Versatile and filling; great for salads or curries.
  • Tofu & Tempeh - Soy-based, high in protein and easy to cook.
  • Quinoa - A complete plant protein with all nine essential amino acids.
  • Nuts & Seeds (Almonds, Peanuts, Chia, Flax) - Add healthy fats + protein.
  • Peas - Another affordable source of plant protein for soups and stir-fries.

Animal-Based Protein Sources


  • Chicken Breast - High in lean protein; great for muscle repair and low in fat.
  • Eggs - Affordable, versatile, and provide all essential amino acids.
  • Greek Yogurt / Skimmed Curd - Packed with protein and probiotics.
  • Fish (Salmon, Tuna, Mackerel) - Rich in protein and healthy omega-3 fats.
  • Lean Red Meat - Provides protein plus iron and vitamin B12.
  • Milk & Cottage Cheese (Paneer) - High in casein protein for slow release.
